FIMbox

Project BriefUpdated May 8, 2026

FIMbox is a modular open-source testbed for Flood Inundation Mapping. It is designed to make FIM workflows more configurable, reproducible, and easier to compare across datasets, model versions, and evaluation methods.

PythonFIMHydroinformaticsResearch Software
ShareLinkedInX

What It Does

  • Organizes FIM experiments into reusable modules.
  • Supports consistent configuration and documentation.
  • Provides a bridge between model generation, benchmarks, and evaluation.
  • Advances research-to-operations (R2O) pathways for NOAA/CIROH by enabling cross-institutional collaboration on standardized FIM pipelines.

Why It Matters

Flood-mapping workflows can become difficult to reproduce when data, scripts, parameters, and evaluation routines are scattered. FIMbox helps package that workflow into a clearer project structure. Its configurable simulation pipelines and standardized APIs make it practical for teams at different institutions to compare results and share components without rebuilding workflows from scratch.
