Applied GIS, Surveying & Mapping Projects

Project BriefUpdated May 9, 2026

Since undergrad, this work has spanned participatory mapping, UAV orthophoto generation, DGPS and field surveying, hydropower and cadastral surveying, humanitarian open mapping, WebGIS visualization, weather prediction, and GDAL-based image analysis.

What It Includes

  • Participatory mapping and mapathon-based open geodata creation in earthquake-affected communities (HOT/OSM).
  • Undergraduate thesis work on predicting temperature and precipitation patterns in Kaski District from past meteorological records using LSTM.
  • Twenty-one-day survey camp work covering DGPS/total station surveying, small hydropower and bridge site mapping in Nepal, and cadastral parcel mapping.
  • UAV orthophoto generation, drone data processing, and campus-scale digitization workflows.
  • WebGIS dashboards, ArcGIS workflows, GDAL-based satellite image analysis, and thematic map production for decision support.

Why It Matters

This section shows the practical GIS and mapping foundation behind the later research work: community mapping, field surveying, drone processing, weather analytics, and map communication. The range of methods — from total station surveys to LSTM-based weather prediction — reflects end-to-end geospatial problem solving where field context, data processing, validation, and visualization all connect.
